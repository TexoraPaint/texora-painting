Port Coquitlam's Trusted Interior Painters

Port Coquitlam — affectionately known as PoCo — is a community of family homes built primarily from the 1980s through the 2000s. Split-levels, two-storey colonials, and side-split layouts dominate neighbourhoods like Mary Hill and Hyde Creek, while Citadel Heights and Riverwood offer more contemporary designs. That 30-to-40-year age range means many PoCo homes have walls that have never seen a professional repaint: scuffed builder-grade flat paint, nail pops, stress cracks at corners, and faded accent walls that don't quite match the current decade's palette. Texora Painting specializes in giving these well-loved family homes the refresh they deserve.

We understand that painting a lived-in family home in Port Coquitlam is different from painting an empty show suite. There are kids' bedrooms with decades of crayon and scuffs on the trim. There are hallways that see daily traffic. There are kitchens where grease has settled into painted surfaces. We prep all of these surfaces properly — cleaning, filling, priming, and where needed, applying a stain-blocking primer before the finish coats — so the final result is durable, beautiful, and genuinely transformed. Our low-VOC paint selections let families return to freshly painted rooms quickly and safely.

Interior painting in Port Coquitlam runs $300–$580 per room with a written quote provided upfront. We work with Benjamin Moore, Sherwin-Williams, and PPG premium lines and include a complimentary colour consultation with every project. Whether you're repainting a single bedroom before a listing, refreshing the whole main floor, or doing a complete top-to-bottom interior overhaul, we handle it with the same care and professionalism.

Our Interior Painting Process

1

Free In-Home Quote

We visit your Port Coquitlam home, assess every surface, discuss colours and finishes, and provide a firm written estimate with no hidden fees.

2

Surface Preparation

We clean walls, fill all holes and cracks, sand smooth, apply stain-blocking primer where needed, and fully protect floors and furniture before painting.

3

Two-Coat Application

Two full coats of premium paint applied with professional tools. Clean edges at every ceiling line, corner, and trim junction — no tape bleed, no roller texture.

4

Walkthrough & Touch-Ups

We inspect every room with you before leaving. Any spots that need attention are touched up immediately — we leave only when you're fully satisfied.
