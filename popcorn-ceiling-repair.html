What Is Popcorn Ceiling Texture?

Popcorn ceiling — also called acoustic ceiling texture, cottage cheese ceiling, or stucco ceiling — was one of the most widely used ceiling finishes in North American homes from the 1960s through the early 1990s. Its appeal was practical: it could be sprayed on quickly and cheaply, it hid imperfections in the drywall underneath, and it was claimed to provide minor acoustic benefits. In Tsawwassen and Ladner, where significant residential development occurred in the 1970s and 1980s, popcorn ceilings are extremely common.

By today's standards, popcorn texture is considered dated and undesirable. Modern home staging advice consistently recommends removing it before listing a property for sale. It traps dust and is nearly impossible to clean. It shows water stains prominently. It makes rooms feel lower and darker. The good news is that Texora Painting removes popcorn ceilings throughout Delta — and the transformation is dramatic.

Critical Safety Notice — Asbestos in Pre-1990 Homes

Popcorn ceiling texture applied before 1990 in BC may contain chrysotile asbestos. This was a common additive in spray-texture products of that era. Homes in Tsawwassen and Ladner built between the 1960s and late 1980s are at particular risk. Disturbing asbestos-containing material without testing and proper abatement is illegal under WorkSafeBC regulations and dangerous to your health. Before any popcorn ceiling removal, have the material tested by a certified asbestos inspector. If asbestos is present, a licensed abatement contractor must remove it. Texora Painting does not work on confirmed asbestos-containing materials — we proceed only after clearance is confirmed.

Patch Repair vs. Full Removal — Which Is Right For You?

Patch Repair

Best for: small areas of physical damage (a water stain, a section knocked by furniture, a hole from a fixture). We spray-match the existing texture and blend the patch into the surrounding area. A perfect match is rarely achievable on aged popcorn — the original material settles and fades over time — but we get as close as possible. If you are not planning to repaint the whole ceiling, a patch repair is the most practical option.

Full Removal

Best for: modernizing your home, preparing for sale, removing heavily damaged or stained popcorn, or addressing asbestos concerns. Full removal gives you a clean slate — smooth, skim-coated ceilings that look like new construction. This is the most impactful single cosmetic upgrade you can make to a 1970s or 1980s home in Delta.

The Popcorn Ceiling Removal Process

Step 1 — Asbestos Testing (Pre-1990 homes)

For homes built before 1990, testing is mandatory before we begin. We can recommend certified asbestos testing services in Delta. Testing typically takes 1–3 business days for results. If asbestos is found, a licensed abatement contractor must remove the material before Texora can proceed.

Step 2 — Furniture Protection and Prep

Popcorn removal is a messy process — the scraped material falls as wet debris and covers everything below. We protect floors, furniture, and all surfaces with plastic sheeting before starting. Proper prep is essential.

Step 3 — Moisten and Scrape

The ceiling is lightly misted with water to soften the texture. Once the texture has absorbed enough moisture to release (usually 15–20 minutes), it is scraped cleanly from the drywall below using wide scrapers. Care is taken not to gouge the drywall face — this would require additional repair work.

Step 4 — Skim Coat

After scraping, the bare drywall surface has minor imperfections, screw dimples, and slight texture from the old material. We apply one to two coats of skim coat compound to create a smooth, flat surface. This is the most skill-dependent step — a smooth ceiling that looks truly flat requires proper feathering and sanding technique.

Step 5 — Prime and Paint

The skim-coated ceiling is primed with drywall primer and painted in your chosen colour and sheen. Flat paint is the standard for ceilings — it hides any minor surface irregularities better than any other sheen level.
