Seamless Drywall Repair Across Port Moody

Port Moody's hillside topography and high rainfall create conditions that are genuinely tough on interior walls. Homes built on sloping lots in Heritage Mountain, College Park, and Glenayre are susceptible to seasonal soil movement that produces diagonal settling cracks in drywall — especially at door and window corners where structural stress concentrates. Meanwhile, Port Moody's above-average annual precipitation means roof leaks, ice damming on older Heritage Mountain homes, and plumbing failures all leave behind water-stained, soft, or bubbled drywall that needs to be properly removed and replaced before you can repaint.

We handle every type of drywall repair Port Moody homeowners encounter. Small nail pops and hairline cracks get filled, reinforced with mesh tape, and feathered so the repair disappears under paint. Medium holes from doorknobs, wall anchors, or plumbing access get patched with new drywall, taped, mudded in three coats, and sanded to a smooth finish. Large areas of water-damaged or mould-affected drywall are cut out entirely, the framing is inspected and dried, new board is installed and finished to match. We also address the textured surfaces common in Port Moody's 1980s and 1990s builds — orange peel, skip trowel, and knockdown all get matched so the repair blends into the room.

Because we're a full painting contractor as well, we don't stop at the drywall repair — we prime and repaint the repaired area to match your existing wall colour, so the job is truly finished when we leave. Most repairs and touch-up painting can be completed in a single visit, which is especially convenient for Port Moody strata owners who want minimal disruption to their day.

Our Drywall Repair Process

1

Assessment & Quote

We inspect the damage, check for underlying moisture or structural causes, and provide a written quote that covers repair, priming, and paint touch-up.

2

Repair & Reinforcement

We cut out damaged sections, install backing or new drywall panels, tape all joints with fibreglass mesh, and apply multiple coats of joint compound.

3

Sand & Texture Match

Once cured, the repair is sanded smooth and any existing wall texture — orange peel, skip trowel, or smooth — is replicated to blend with the surrounding surface.

4

Prime & Paint

We prime the repaired area and repaint to match the existing wall colour, leaving the wall looking as if nothing was ever there.
