What You Need to Know

Our Cabinet Painting Process

We remove all doors and hardware, degrease every surface, sand thoroughly, apply a bonding primer, then spray 2 coats of cabinet-specific enamel with a hardener for maximum durability. Doors are sprayed flat for a flawless finish.

Why Spray vs. Brush & Roller?

Brushed cabinets show texture, lap marks, and brush strokes — especially on flat door fronts. Spray application lays paint atomized and even, giving you the same finish quality as a factory. It's not optional for us — it's standard.

Will It Hold Up?

With proper prep, primer, and a hardener-added topcoat, painted cabinets are extremely durable. They resist chips, yellowing, and moisture — the same properties as factory-finished cabinets.

Choosing Your Colour

White and off-white are the most popular — they brighten the space and photograph beautifully. But we'll spray any colour you love and can match virtually any colour chip from any brand.
