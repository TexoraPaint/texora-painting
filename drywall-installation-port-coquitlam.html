Drywall Installation for Port Coquitlam Renovations & Developments

Port Coquitlam's family homes are some of the most heavily renovated in the Tri-Cities area. With large lots, split-level and two-storey layouts, and unfinished basements that represent thousands of square feet of untapped living space, PoCo homeowners are constantly developing, expanding, and updating. Drywall installation is at the centre of nearly every one of these projects — and getting it done right the first time is what separates a renovation that looks polished and professional from one that looks like a DIY project.

We work on all types of drywall installation projects in Port Coquitlam: full basement developments where the entire below-grade level is being converted to livable space, individual room additions where a garage or bonus room is being enclosed, feature walls and entertainment niches in main-floor renovations, and ceiling replacements where outdated popcorn or damaged drywall needs to be removed and replaced. Each scenario has its own board requirements, finishing demands, and code considerations — moisture-resistant board in bathrooms and laundry rooms, fire-rated Type X in utility and furnace rooms, standard board everywhere else.

Our drywall installation pricing in Port Coquitlam runs $800–$2,500 per room, which includes hanging, taping, mudding in multiple coats, corner bead, sanding, and a prime-coat-ready Level 4 finish. If you want Level 5 (full skim coat) for a perfectly smooth wall under flat paint or wallpaper, we offer that as an upgrade. We can also paint the completed walls and ceilings as part of the same project — one crew, zero scheduling headaches.

Our Drywall Installation Process

1

Site Visit & Quote

We assess the framing, measure the space, identify board type requirements, and provide a written quote covering all labour and materials for your PoCo project.

2

Board Selection & Hanging

Right board for every location — moisture-resistant, fire-rated, or standard — cut precisely and screwed firmly to studs or ceiling joists at the correct spacing.

3

Tape, Mud & Sand

All joints and screw holes taped and mudded in three coats with full drying and sanding between each. Corner bead installed and finished on all outside corners.

4

Level 4 Finish & Prime

Final sand to a Level 4 (or Level 5 on request) standard. Primer coat applied — walls are ready for your paint colour the day we complete the job.
