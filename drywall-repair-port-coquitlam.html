Professional Drywall Repair Across Port Coquitlam

Homes built in Port Coquitlam during the 1980s and 1990s construction boom are now showing their age in one very specific way: drywall problems. Nail pops are the most common issue — as framing lumber dries and shrinks over decades, nails that were set flush at construction get pushed back through the paper face of the drywall, creating small raised bumps or even punctured paint. Stress cracks at door corners, window corners, and ceiling-wall junctions are the next most common issue, caused by the normal movement of a wood-framed home through seasonal temperature cycles. And then there are the holes: doorknob impacts, TV mounting attempts, plumbing access that was never properly patched.

Texora Painting handles all categories of drywall repair in Port Coquitlam — small holes and nail pops, medium-sized damage sections, water-stained areas, and larger cut-out repairs where new backing and a patch panel are required. The key difference between our repairs and a DIY attempt is texture matching and feathering. We don't just slap compound over a hole and sand it flat — we identify the texture pattern on your wall (orange peel, skip trowel, or smooth), replicate it precisely over the repair, prime with a stain-blocking primer, and leave a surface that's genuinely invisible once painted.

Drywall repair pricing in Port Coquitlam starts at $150 for a single patch and runs to $350 for more complex or larger repairs. Grouping multiple repairs in one visit is the most cost-effective approach — we charge a single trip fee and then price each additional repair incrementally. Many PoCo homeowners book us for a pre-listing repair session to address every imperfection before real estate photos.

Our Drywall Repair Process

1

Assessment & Quote

We view all the repair locations in your Port Coquitlam home, identify the repair method needed for each, and give you a firm price before any work starts.

2

Repair & Backing

Nail pops are re-set or screwed, holes are backed with mesh or a new drywall patch panel, and cracks are widened slightly then properly taped with joint compound.

3

Mud, Texture & Sand

Multiple coats of joint compound are applied and feathered, texture is matched to the surrounding wall, then sanded smooth between coats and after the final coat.

4

Prime & Paint-Ready

Stain-blocking primer is applied over every repair. The surface is left smooth, primed, and ready for your chosen wall colour — invisible in any lighting.
